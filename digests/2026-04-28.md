## Deep Dive of the Day

**Topic: Harness design and the session/harness/sandbox decoupling for long-horizon agents.**

This is the single most interview-relevant concept on today's feed. FAANG agent interviews increasingly probe how you reason about long-running, multi-context-window agents — and the industry has now converged on a clear architectural answer. 
Managed Agents virtualize the components of an agent: a session (the append-only log of everything that happened), a harness (the loop that calls Claude and routes tool calls), and a sandbox.
 The deep insight is that 
the space of interesting harness combinations doesn't shrink as models improve — it moves, and the interesting work for AI engineers is to keep finding the next novel combination.
 Worth digging deeper into: (1) event-log-as-context patterns (getEvents-style positional slicing vs naive history compaction), (2) credential isolation via MCP proxy + vault so the harness never sees tokens, (3) the build-vs-buy economics now that 
teams can benchmark internal harnesses against Anthropic Managed Agents at eight cents per session hour plus tokens, or against OpenAI's open-source SDK with no first-party runtime fee
, and (4) failure modes like "context anxiety" and how harness-level interventions (context resets, initializer agents, claude-progress.txt-style state files) compensate for them. Read both Anthropic engineering posts back-to-back; together they form the canonical 2026 mental model for production agent infrastructure.
